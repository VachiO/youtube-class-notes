# Audit Lecture Summary Completeness

Compare the revised transcript with the current lecture summary.

Revised transcript:

```text
{{REVISED_TRANSCRIPT}}
```

Current summary:

```text
{{CURRENT_SUMMARY}}
```

Your task is to identify and restore information that is missing or overly compressed.

Check the transcript sequentially from beginning to end.

Look especially for missing:

- Main concepts and definitions
- Separate arguments or steps in the teacher's reasoning
- Examples, cases, comparisons, analogies, names, and events
- Causes, effects, conditions, exceptions, and qualifications
- Questions asked by the teacher or students and their answers
- Side knowledge and background explanations
- Stories, anecdotes, personal experiences, and meaningful off-topic discussion from the teacher
- Teacher opinions, emphasis, warnings, and memorable wording
- Exam scope, likely questions, required reading, and memorization instructions
- Assignments, quizzes, scores, deadlines, attendance, training, meetings, and university agenda
- Details near the end of each transcript section

Treat side knowledge, stories, and meaningful digressions as valuable lecture content. Do not exclude them merely because they are outside the main topic. Restore them when they provide context, illustrate an idea, reveal the teacher's reasoning or experience, or add useful general knowledge.

Do not merely report that something is missing.

Output replacement or additional Thai lecture-note prose that can be merged into the current summary. Preserve the approximate position and order of the missing material.

Do not invent information. Do not shorten existing information. If no meaningful information is missing, output exactly:

```text
COMPLETE
```
